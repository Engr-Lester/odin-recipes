Sinigang Recipe

SINIGANG: Deliciously made with love

A Filipino favorite. A comforting, tamarind-based sour soup loaded with tender proteins, crisp garden vegetables, and signature savory notes. Perfectly paired with steamed white rice.

Ingredients

  • pork belly or ribs
  • tamarind-based broth
  • tomatoes
  • onions
  • kangkong, radish, eggplant, okra, sitaw
  • fish sauce
  • green chili

How to Cook

  1. Place your choice of protein (e.g., pork ribs or belly) in a large pot with enough water to cover it completely.
  2. Bring to a boil and meticulously skim off the scum (impurities) that rises to the top for a clear broth.
  3. Once the broth is clear, add quartered onions and tomatoes.
  4. Lower the heat and simmer until the meat is fork-tender. This typically takes 45 to 90 minutes for pork.
  5. If using gabi (taro), add it early enough to soften; some cooks prefer to mash a few pieces to thicken the broth.
  6. Add "harder" vegetables like radish (labanos) and eggplant (talong), simmering for about 5 minutes.
  7. Add string beans (sitaw), okra, and long green chili (siling haba). Cook for another 3–5 minutes until tender but still slightly crisp.
  8. Stir in your sinigang mix or fresh tamarind extrac
  9. Season with fish sauce (patis) and black pepper to achieve your desired balance of sour and salty.
  10. Finally, add the kangkong (water spinach) or other leafy greens.

    11. Pro Tip: You can turn off the heat and cover the pot; the residual heat will perfectly wilt the leaves in 1–2 minutes without overcooking them.
